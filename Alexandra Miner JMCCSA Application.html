Dear Mr. Wilcox,

As an educator who is deeply committed to fostering an equitable learning environment, I am eager to contribute to your multidisciplinary team as an Occasional Teacher. With a focus on supporting students with diverse learning and motivational needs, I am particularly drawn to the unique challenges and opportunities that arise in schools dedicated to providing specialized education alongside rehabilitation and treatment services. Throughout my career, I have embraced the importance of individualized support and meaningful engagement for all students. My experiences supporting people with exceptionalities, including roles at Community Living, Autism Services Inc., and the Windsor Essex Catholic District School Board has equipped me with tools and insight into creating and implementing Individualized Education Plans tailored to meet the unique needs of each student. These experiences have reinforced my belief that child-centered and inclusive education is imperative in providing students with their fundamental right to quality education. Currently, I am contributing to the educational community as an occasional teacher with the Windsor Essex Catholic District School Board. I was grateful to teach a determined student living with physical and developmental needs during a long-term assignment. My experience with this student refined my methods, focusing on ensuring students could access the curriculum meaningfully and recognize their integral role in our classroom community. Overall, this role provided me with valuable insights into the educational landscape of our community, revealing the critical need for inclusivity and equity in educational programs to ensure all students can thrive.

Thank you for considering my application. I am excited about the possibility of contributing to the continued success and growth of John McGivney Children’s Centre School Authority. I look forward to the opportunity to discuss how my experiences and skills align with the needs of your school community.

To Whole It May Concern,

Please accept this letter of reference for Alexandra Miner. Allie completed a challenging long term teaching assignment from November 9, 2022 to June 16, 2023 as a Grade 8 Teacher during the 2022/2023 school year at St. Gabriel School. This position was constantly evolving from month to month. The teacher was expected back to work but as time progressed, Allie found herself immersed in the role as the full time classroom teacher for the majority of the school year. She accommodated all that was asked of her for the consistency and well being of her students.

Allie worked extremely hard at St. Gabriel school. She was put into a position where she had to formally assess student work and complete report cards for the first time after only knowing her students for a short time. She embraced this challenge and did an exceptional job. She was put in a position where she had to participate in parent-teacher interviews, grade 8 transition meetings, graduation preparation and a schoolwide activity day. Allie also completed the June report cards for her grade 8 students. Her comments, structure and professionalism were that of a veteran teacher.

While Allie did a great job performing the day to day duties of a grade teacher, her focus was always on curriculum and delivering the program to meet the needs of all her students. She spent many long hours preparing for each day, oftentimes coming in on weekends to be prepared for the week ahead. Allie is an extremely hard worker and does a great job with planning. She was never hesitant to reach out for assistance with respect to assessment and evaluation, the new MathUp resource or strategies on managing behaviors in the classroom. She managed her classroom very well and was eager to learn new ideas from the veteran teachers and support staff around her which are the assets of a true professional!

Allie deserves a great deal of credit for her accomplishments as a grade 8 teacher this school year. She handled herself with professionalism and demonstrated genuine care for her students. Her students respected her and she was able to handle all parent meetings with grace and professionalism. Allie’s attendance at the grade 8 graduation (after her assignment was complete) did not go unnoticed by staff, parents and most importantly, her students.

Allie has demonstrated that she is an individual who cares about her profession, her professional development and the well-being of her students. She has an extremely strong work ethic and a passion for student learning. I strongly recommend Allie Miner for a full time teaching position within our board.

Please do not hesitate to contact me should you have any questions with respect to Alexandra Miner at 519-969-3230 or bob_flanagan@wecdsb.on.ca.

Sincerely,
